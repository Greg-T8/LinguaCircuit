### succinct (vocabulary)

- **Definition:** Expressed clearly and in few words; brief but comprehensive.
- **Part of speech:** adjective
- **Difficulty:** 2/5
- **Status:** new | **Due:** 2026-03-21 | **Ease:** 2.5
- **Etymology:** From Latin 'succinctus' — tucked up, girded. 'sub-' (under) + 'cingere' (to gird). Metaphor of being tightly bound.
- **Examples:**
  - His succinct summary captured the key points in under a minute.
  - Keep your emails succinct — busy people appreciate brevity.
- **Commonly confused with:**
  - **concise:** Nearly synonymous, but 'succinct' emphasizes compression; 'concise' emphasizes removal of excess.
  - **terse:** 'Terse' can imply rudeness or abruptness; 'succinct' is always positive.
- **Usage notes:**
  - Positive connotation — implies efficiency, not lack of depth
  - Opposite of verbose or long-winded
  - Valued in professional communication, technical writing, and presentations
